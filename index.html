The Round Table

Many times districts are unresponsive to studentâ€™s requests for a change in curriculum to include a more culturally diverse and socially inclusive curriculum to help foster a culturally knowledgeable and responsive school community. As a result, many school clubs are advocating for cultural and social diversity in curriculum and in school settings. However, many of these clubs may be working individually and therefore have a limited sphere of communication due to differences in social and cultural identities. Finally, there are not many educators that are interested in addressing this issue due to their own busy schedules or because efforts toward diversity and inclusion in education still lack reputation.

The overall purpose of this project is to create a network of a variety of different social and cultural identity clubs in order to facilitate sharing of resources along with important information. An outcome of this the resolving of common misconceptions between such clubs in order to foster culturally and socially cognizant school communities from around San Diego. The ultimate goal is to provide a safe and welcoming space for all members of different social and cultural identity organizations. This means having individual meetings in order to present a certain topic and request resources, and also having group meetings in order to promote resources and ideas.

In order to achieve this, San Diego County of Education, SDCOE, has offered to aid in the promotion of the project and help in interconnecting different districts to enable this project to expand to the whole San Diego County. Due to this aid, this project will inevitably gain some amount of reputation. Through our invitation of educators, we can help make educators more interested in learning about the concerns of students from various social and cultural backgrounds and addressing those concerns in the classroom.
